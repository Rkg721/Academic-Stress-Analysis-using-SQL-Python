Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis of academic stress among 140 students reveals critical insights into the drivers, impacts, and potential interventions for student well-being. Our findings indicate that undergraduate students experience the highest stress levels, with home pressure (family expectations) and peer pressure serving as the primary contributing factors.


The data shows a concerning upward trend in stress levels over time, with significant variations based on study environment and coping strategies employed. Students utilizing analytical problem-solving approaches demonstrate 23% lower stress levels compared to those experiencing emotional breakdowns as their primary response mechanism.

Key Finding: Home pressure emerges as the most significant stressor, rating higher than peer pressure and academic competition combined. This insight necessitates immediate parental engagement initiatives and family support programs.
